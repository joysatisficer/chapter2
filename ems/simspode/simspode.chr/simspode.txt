Language modelling requires modelling the process that generated the text. In other words, a language model has a world model. Because text fragments don't contain the full context and history, language models also implicitly predict a superposition of the present and past of the situation the text describes. In order to generate more text, language models model possible future outcomes and simulate how the world evolves. For this reason, they are also known as "simulators" among the members of Cyborgism.
